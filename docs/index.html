Your AI starts over.
Every. Single. Chat.

Until now. One install. Every session after, your AI arrives knowing what happened last time — what you're building, what failed, how you like to work.

The problem

Every time you open a new chat with an AI assistant, it starts from zero. It doesn't remember what you worked on last week, what decisions you made together, or what failed last time. You're not building a working relationship. You're briefing a new contractor every single time.

How it works
Three pieces. Installed once.
The Playbook Memory

A shared document that both you and your AI update after every session. It holds the things a good collaborator would remember: what you're working on, what happened last time, what approaches failed, how you like to work. Your AI reads it first before doing anything. You can read and edit it too. It's just text.

The Rules Behavior

By default, your AI hedges everything. It shows you options and asks you to choose. It qualifies every answer. The rules change that. Claude commits — to a decision, to an approach, to an answer. You get direct responses that move work forward, not polite suggestions that put the thinking back on you.

The Gate Enforcement

A small program that runs silently every time you send a message. Before your AI writes a single word, it has to name the one concrete thing it's delivering. This stops the most common failure: a long, careful, safe response that technically answers your question but never commits to anything.

The outcome

Less like a tool.
More like a teammate who was actually there.

Plain text. One install. Yours to adapt.
