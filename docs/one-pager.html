loop-closed

Your AI starts over.
Every. Single. Chat.

loop-closed is a small system that changes that. One install. Every session after that, your AI shows up knowing what happened last time.

The problem

Every time you open a new chat with an AI assistant, it starts from zero. It doesn't remember what you worked on last week, what decisions you made together, or what failed the last time. You're not building a working relationship. You're briefing a new contractor every single time.

How it works
Three pieces. Installed once.
01
The Playbook Memory

A shared document that both you and your AI update after every session. It holds the things a good collaborator would remember: what you're working on, what happened last time, what approaches failed, how you like to work. Your AI reads it first before doing anything. You can read and edit it too. It's just text.

02
The Rules Guardrails

A small set of behavioral guardrails. Not suggestions. Structural constraints. Things like: don't show me three options and ask me to pick. Make the decision. Don't touch anything I didn't ask you to touch. If you're about to hedge with "you might want to consider," stop. Say what you actually think.

03
The Gate Enforcement

A small program that fires silently every time you send a message. Before your AI writes a single word, it has to name the one concrete thing it's delivering. This stops the most common failure: a long, careful, safe response that technically answers your question but never commits to anything.

The outcome

Claude starts feeling less like a tool.
More like a collaborator who was actually there last week.

The system is a starting point, not a product. Fork it, make it yours. The playbook adapts to how you work, not the other way around.
